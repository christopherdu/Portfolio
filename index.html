I'm Christopher Du,
a front-end developer based in Toronto.

I went to Seneca College to study digital media where I found my passion for web development. I discovered front-end development was what I wanted to pursue through the unique experience offered by it that other programming languages didn't offer. The mixture of creativity and problem solving unique to front-end development is something that I thoroughly enjoy engaging with. My ultimate goal as a developer is to be creating products that users enjoy using. Front-end development is such an important aspect of the internet that I want to be fully able to understand it, so I can create things that have some kind of value to someone, somewhere.

Outside of web development I would consider myself to be an introverted person, usually engaging in hobbies that allow for some alone time. Some of these hobbies include playing video games, watching and analyzing movies, listening to music, and reading fiction. I find the time I get to spend alone refreshes my mind, and puts me in a state of serenity and introspection. It allows me to really take in and absorb everything that's been happening to me prior. Isolation, self reflection, and introspection are things that I value really highly in life. Especially in an age where we're in constant communication, with very few lapses in time where an individual can take time to self reflect.
